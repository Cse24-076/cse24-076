FC Barcelona's Passionate Supporters

The nickname "culer" for a Barcelona supporter comes from Catalan "cul" (meaning "backside"). This originated at the club's first stadium, Camp de la Indústria, where spectators sat with their backsides hanging over the stands. Today, this term is worn with pride by Barça fans worldwide.

FC Barcelona boasts one of the largest global followings in sports. The club has over 103 million Facebook fans (as of 2021), ranking second among all sports teams worldwide. Throughout Europe, Barcelona is consistently among the most popular "second-choice" clubs for neutral football fans.

Un Dia De Partit - Match Day Experience

Match days at Camp Nou are more than just football - they're a cultural experience. From the morning build-up in local bars to the post-match celebrations, Culés create an atmosphere unlike any other in world football.

Match Day Traditions

Senyeras & Esteladas

The waving of Catalan flags (senyeras) and independence flags (esteladas) has become a prominent feature at Camp Nou, especially during important matches. This tradition reflects the club's deep connection with Catalan identity.

Cant del Barça

The club anthem is sung passionately before every match, with the entire stadium joining in. The anthem, written in Catalan, was composed in 1974 and remains a powerful expression of club identity.

Scarf Display

During important European nights, fans participate in coordinated scarf displays, creating stunning visual mosaics (called "mosaics" or "tifos") that often carry messages of support or Catalan pride.

Pre-match Meals

Many local fans follow the tradition of eating at specific restaurants or bars before matches, with establishments near the stadium offering special "menú del partido" (match day menus).

Club Membership

The club's membership has seen dramatic growth, from 100,000 in 2003-04 to 170,000 in 2009. This surge was attributed to Ronaldinho's popularity and Joan Laporta's media strategy. While membership fluctuates, the socis remain the heart of the club's ownership structure.
