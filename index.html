About FC Barcelona

FC Barcelona, affectionately known as Barça, is more than just a football club - it's a symbol of Catalan identity and pride. Founded in 1899 by Swiss businessman Joan Gamper along with a group of football enthusiasts, the club has grown to become one of the most beloved and successful sports institutions in the world.

The club's motto "Més que un club" ("More than a club") reflects its deep roots in Catalan culture and its role as an ambassador for the region. Owned by its supporters (socis), Barcelona represents values of democracy, social commitment, and sporting excellence.

Barcelona competes in La Liga, Spain's top football division, and has won numerous domestic and international titles, including 5 UEFA Champions League trophies and 27 La Liga championships. The club is renowned for its youth academy, La Masia, which has produced legendary players like Lionel Messi, Xavi, and Andrés Iniesta.

Beyond football, FC Barcelona maintains professional teams in basketball, handball, roller hockey, and other sports, all competing under the same colors and values that make the club unique in the world of sports.
