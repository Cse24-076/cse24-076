Camp Nou

The largest stadium in Europe and home of FC Barcelona since 1957

The Temple of Football

Camp Nou, meaning "New Field" in Catalan, is more than just a stadium - it's a symbol of Catalan identity and one of the most iconic football venues in the world. With a seating capacity of 99,354, it's the largest stadium in Europe and the third-largest football stadium in the world.

The stadium was inaugurated on September 24, 1957, and has since hosted countless historic matches, including two Champions League finals, the 1982 World Cup, and the 1992 Olympic Games football tournament.

VIP & Hospitality Experience

Camp Nou offers exclusive VIP areas that combine luxury with the passion of football. These premium spaces provide unparalleled views of the pitch along with gourmet dining and premium services.

The most exclusive area, reserved for club officials, visiting dignitaries, and special guests. Features private lounges and the best views in the stadium.

Sky Box

Premium restaurant with panoramic views of the pitch, offering gourmet Catalan cuisine before and during matches.

Stadium History & Future

Camp Nou's story is intertwined with the history of FC Barcelona and the city itself:

1954

Construction begins on September 14, with architect Francesc Mitjans leading the project.

1957

Official inauguration on September 24 with a match between FC Barcelona and Warsaw selection.

1982

Hosts matches for the World Cup, including the opening game and a semifinal.

1992

Olympic football tournament held at Camp Nou during the Barcelona Olympics.

1998-99

Major renovation increases capacity to over 98,000 and earns 5-star UEFA rating.

2022-26

Espai Barça project transforms the stadium with a roof, increased capacity to 105,000, and modern facilities.
